UGA Football History

  • 1892 - University of Georgia's Inaugural Game at Herty Field against Mercer. Georgia would win the game with the final score of 50-0
  • 1895 - Founding member of the Southern Intercollegiate Athletic Association (SIAA). The SIAA was one of the first athletic conferences in the US
  • 1896 - Wins first Conference Championship!
  • 1920 - Nickname "Bulldogs" adopted despite earlier mentions dating back to 1901
  • 1921 - Departs from the SIAA to become a founding member for the Southern Conference
  • 1929 - Georgia plays it's first game at Sanford Stadium
  • 1933 - Departs from the Southern Conference to become a founding member for the Southeastern Conference where they would remain a member to present day
  • 1942 - Wins first SEC Championship!
  • 1942 - National Champions!
  • 1942 - Frank Sinkwich becomes the first UGA Heisman Trophy winner
  • 1943 - English Bulldog known as "Uga" makes his debut
  • 1980 - National Champions!
  • 1980 - Herschel Walker wins the Heisman Trophy
  • 2021 - National Champions!
  • 2022 - Sets NFL Draft record for most defensive players selected in the first round (5)
  • 2022 - Sets NFL Draft record for most players selected (15)

College football in Athens, GA has grown to become one of the nation's most prestigious programs. The program has accumulated fifteen (15) conference championships, is second all-time in bowl wins, and has seen eighty-four (84) players selected to the All-American Team. Eighteen (18) members of the Georgia Bulldogs would be inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame and currently five (5) are seated in the Pro Football Hall of Fame.

The most notable and accomplished Bulldogs would go on to be immortalized and their jersey numbers would never be worn again. Frank Sinkwich was a 2-time All-American and the first Heisman Trophy winner at the University of Georgia and the Southeastern Conference. Sinkwich would also lead Georgia to its first claimed National Championship in 1942. Herschel Walker, considered one of the best college football players of all-time, is a 3-time All-American and Heisman Trophy winner. As a first-year student, Walker would lead the Bulldogs to a National Championship and go on to hold many rushing records over his career. Theron Sapp, also known as the Drought Breaker, played a vital role in overcoming Georgia's rivals. Sapp would go on to earn All-SEC honors as a fullback. Charley Trippi, known for his versatility, played practically every position on the field. He would help Georgia win the National Championship in 1942 as the Rose Bowl MVP. Trippi would go on to become an All-American, win the Maxwell Award, and the SEC Player of the Year. Frank Sinkwich, Herschel Walker, and Charley Trippi would all go on to be inducted in the College Football Hall of Fame.

Rivalries

The Georgia Bulldogs have many storied rivalries. The "Deep South's Oldest Rivalry" against the Auburn Tigers date back to their inaugural season in 1892. And the legendary "Clean, Old-Fashioned Hate" rivalry versus the Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets would kick off the following year in 1893. Arguably their biggest rivalry started back in the early 1900's against the Florida Gators. The game has been played every year in Jacksonville, FL since 1933. It has come to be known as the "World's Largest Outdoor Cocktail Party". Of the eight rivalry games that Georgia participates in, they lead their all-time series in seven of them.

  • Versus Auburn - Georgia leads the series
  • Versus Clemson - Georgia leads the series
  • Versus Florida - Georgia leads the series
  • Versus Georgia Tech - Georgia leads the series
  • Versus South Carolina - Georgia leads the series
  • Versus Tennessee - Georgia leads the series
  • Versus Vanderbilt - Georgia leads the series
  • Versus Alabama - Alabama leads the series
